# Archive Ontologically

Relocates an asset into its **archive ontology** — a homoiconic, single-asset,
idempotent archival that is an *emergent composition* of already-shipped
grounding primitives (RFC 78c2b7d0 C5). No bespoke archive subsystem.

Composite ([[ee48c626-ce92-43f9-b7b6-b099cdf014b2|Archive Ontologically (composite)]])
of two ordered steps:

1. **Re-anchor** `exo__Asset_isDefinedBy` ([[af5090bf-304f-4670-acd5-ab5a42f76bd8|step 1]],
   `property_set`) — value computed by a NamedQuery
   ([[5e2210f4-ab44-402c-b1b7-bd4dfb364301|Resolve archive ontology]]) 1-hop:
   `$currentAsset --isDefinedBy→ srcOnto --exo__Ontology_archiveOntology→ archiveOnto`
   (the C4 read-side `targetValueQuery` CQRS bridge).
2. **Relocate** the file ([[08da54cf-a587-4cf2-a144-ea195fd6cf66|step 2]], `service_call`
   `serviceId=repairFolder`) into the archive ontology's folder — the
   co-location invariant.

- **Precondition**: [[9b0fdc10-ccb9-46b0-80b8-d9a250a01c9b|Archived and not yet ontologically archived]] —
  `archived: true` (stage-1 toggle-archived) **AND** the current ontology declares
  an `exo__Ontology_archiveOntology`. Once relocated, the asset lives in the archive
  ontology (which declares no archive of its own) → the ASK fails → idempotent no-op.
- **CLI**: `apply archive-ontologically <target>`.
